Horse Riding

About

Exploring the coastline on horseback is such a unique way to experience the beauty of the area. The ride along Playa Conchal is especially stunning—it's no wonder this beach was named one of the top 20 most beautiful in the world! The soft sand beneath the horses’ hooves, the vibrant blue waters, and the lush greenery create a gorgeous setting that makes this tour truly special.

Over an hour and a half, you'll ride along the beach and even under some shady trees, which offers a nice break from the Costa Rican heat. Whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced rider, this tour welcomes all levels. The horses are well cared for, and the guides are as passionate about this beautiful spot as we are, ensuring you have an unforgettable experience. If you're looking for a peaceful adventure with incredible views, this is the perfect way to spend a morning or afternoon.

Frequently asked questions

How long is the tour?

The ride is about 1.5 hours—just the right amount of time to soak up the beauty of the beach and catch some shade under the trees. Trust us, you’ll love every minute of it!

Do I need to have riding experience?

No worries! This tour is perfect for everyone, whether it’s your first time on a horse or you’ve ridden before. The guides are super friendly and make sure you feel comfortable and confident throughout the ride.

What should I bring?

We recommend bringing sunscreen, bug spray, a cap, and a bottle of water. Long pants are a good idea to avoid any rubbing from the saddle, and closed-toe shoes are better than flip-flops here!

Don’t worry about carrying too much, though; there are small bags on the saddle pads for your essentials, so you can leave the backpack behind and focus on the adventure.

Are the horses well taken care of?

Absolutely! The horses are gentle, well-loved, and in great health. Nicole, the owner, takes excellent care of them, so you can relax and enjoy the ride with a happy horse.

What’s the best time of day for the ride?

We love heading out early in the morning or later in the afternoon, when the temperatures are cooler and the lighting makes the scenery even more magical.
